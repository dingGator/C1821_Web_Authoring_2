The Guidelines of Florida State Parks

Devils Millhopper, Florida State Parks, with your help, can protect the natural environments and help you have safe visits to Florida State Parks.

Safety Rules of Devils Millhopper

Florida Park Service goal is provide recreational activities while preserving the natural environments of the parks. Please do not disturb wildlife, for both your safety and the safety of the natural habitat.


Guidlines for Devils Millhopper

Hiking: Please be well supplied and fully prepared for your hike. And be aware of your surroundings. And please know what to do if any medical emergency occurs during your hike.
For more hiking safety tips, please visit     www.floridatrail.org

"Pets: Pets are allowed in day-use areas. They must be on handheld leash of six feet or shorter at all time. Owner are required to clean after their pets."
"Service animal: Service animals are "dogs that are individually trained to do work or perform tasks for people with disabilities, according to the     www.ada.gov/service_animals_2010


Service Animal Guidlines for Devils Millhopper

" Our Pet Policy applies to service with the following exeception:
1. Service animals in a working capacity are allowed in all public areas in the park when accompanied by a visitor with a disability; and
2. Service animals should be harnessed, leashed or tethered unless such devices interfere with the service animal's work, or if the visitor's disability prevents the use of these devices. In that case, the visitor must maintain control of the animal through voice, signal or other effective controls.
We understand that service animals may need recreational breaks; therefore if a service animal is not working (e.g., running around, playing fetch, etc.), it is only permitted at designated pet locations."
